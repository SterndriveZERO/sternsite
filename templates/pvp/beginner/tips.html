⌚ Practice time

The amount of time you practice is entirely up to you and what your goal is. If your goal is extremely high (like HT1), I recommend playing for at least four hours per day — obviously that is an extreme amount of playtime, but I believe it is necessary if you do not want to grind one mode for multiple years. I have over 1000 hours in Minecraft PvP overall, and the highest I ever got was HT3 SMP.

🎯 Practicing

Many people ask where to practice, or how to practice. Personally, I believe the best way to improve is to fight someone of equal or higher skill than you — a lot. That helps you understand how people are beating you and what to change so they are not beating you as hard, or if they are around your level you can find ways to push past them.

⚠️ Common mistakes

The most common mistake I see is people believing they cannot reach a certain skill level. It is always possible to improve; you have to find the way. If you do not believe you can improve, you may stop looking for ways to get better.

Another mistake is playing every day for years and getting stuck at a certain tier. That is often from fighting people who are worse than you. For example: you are LT4, but you fight HT5s and LT5s all day. You keep winning and never expose yourself to situations where you have to learn new counters to reach LT3.

🪀 Fun

Another common mistake is refusing to play a certain way because it feels “boring” or not fun. To counter a specific playstyle, ping, or skillset, you sometimes have to play in a way that does not feel enjoyable.

Remember: Minecraft is a block game meant for entertainment. If you force a style that makes you hate the game, you will not have fun and your mindset can hold back improvement — so keep having fun in practice. In tier tests, though, if you are genuinely dedicated to improving, it may be necessary to play a specific way.
