🎯 Core Idea

Sword PvP is not just aim. It is a combination of mechanics that control fights, spacing, timing, and adaptation.

📊 Core Components

🎯 Aim

One of the most important skills in PvP. Determines consistency in hitting opponents.

🏹 Range Control

Understanding when you are in or out of hit range to land hits or avoid damage.

🥏 Spacing

Managing distance to set up stun hits or avoid being punished.

⏱️ Timing

Knowing when to hit, and when to use specific techniques or counters.

🥊 KB Control

Managing knockback using techniques like NoKb or deciding when to disengage.

🪃 Combo Control

Maintaining combos through correct spacing, timing, and technique usage.

🌪️ Movement

Using movement to dodge hits or create optimal spacing for attacks.

🧠 Knowledge

Understanding techniques, matchups, and playstyles.

🎖️ Adaptation

Adjusting your playstyle based on opponent behavior, ping, and strategy.

🏆 Prediction

Reading your opponent before they act and countering their decisions.

🔻 Adaptation Explained

🧑‍🏫 Simple Adaptation

Reacting to one specific habit, such as countering repeated NoKb crit attempts.

🧠 Advanced Adaptation

Recognizing repeated patterns in movement, timing, or trades and adjusting accordingly.

⚔️ Playstyle Adaptation

Changing your overall playstyle to counter a specific opponent.

🪃 Baits

Baiting is the act of forcing your opponent into a predictable move, then countering it.

  • Run forward → bait aggression → step back → punish miss
  • Fake a crit → bait deflection → use falling sprinthit instead
