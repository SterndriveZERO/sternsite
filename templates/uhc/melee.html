⚔️ Melee

❗ Melee consists of using your Sword and Axe in UHC. This will teach you how to use it to the best of your advantage.

⚔️ Agroing

Your agros rely heavily on your crits, movement, and spacing. This means putting your opponent in a position where they are under significant pressure.

🪓 Axe Use

In UHC, the Axe and Sword do similar damage, with it occasionally doing 1/2 a heart more, unlike every other mode. However, the major difference is that the Axe only has Sharpness 1, while the Sword has Sharpness 4. To most, this makes no difference, however, when you are doing the cooldown trick, it can change your damages completely.

💥 Normal Cooldown Trick

The normal cooldown trick will be when you are holding your sword, and then swap to your axe and immediately click. This will make it so that you can disable a shield and get damage in. So if you go for their shield, but they don’t put it up, you’ll do damage to them. If they do put it up, you’ll disable their shield. However, because of the difference in sharpness levels and your use of the cooldown trick, it will only make your sword do the damage of a Sharpness 1 sword, despite it being Sharp 4. In most cases this is negligible, because you do manage to disable their shield.

If you don’t understand how that works, the cooldown trick will take the base damage of the first item, and then apply the properties and enchants of the second.

💥 Extra Damage Cooldown Trick

UHC is the only mode this works in, due to every other mode having the same level of sharpness on its axe. Essentially, this is the opposite of the normal cooldown trick. You hold out your axe, and then swap to your sword, and immediately click. This, will make you do the damage of a Sharpness 4 axe. This is important because it means you can do half a heart more damage. While some may consider this negligible, it means you can hit a 2 heart crit. This can secure drops onto opponents who otherwise would have lived. Be aware that not all hits will be a 2 heart crit, some may be 1.5 heart crits.

💔 Punishing Heals

A large amount of the advantage you get in UHC comes from how you fight your opponent, specifically when they are healing. While there is a lot of ways to gain an advantage in a trade, a massive amount of the game changes based off of healing, and punishing bad healing is the best thing you can do to gain an advantage over your opponent. This can be an absolute game changer, and put them down a ton of health and gaps.
